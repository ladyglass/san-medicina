Pro-Abortion Protest Rocks the Suburbs

by Paul Saylor

About a dozen protesters marched outside of the Women’s Informed Choice Center in West Bement Saturday, raising their voices against what they perceive as a gross miscarriage of justice. The subject of their ire? Abortion and women’s access to it.

Protests on the issue are nothing new. However, this one is managing to turn more than the usual number of heads. The characters are colorful, their perspectives not what you might expect.

“Hey, hey! Ho, ho! That fetus has got to go!”

The Los Angeles-based organization has filed paperwork to become a tax-exempt, non-profit religious corporation. Calling itself The First Church of Cessation, their stated goal is “A cleaner, healthier planet through negative population growth.”

“Obviously, we’re here at this so-called Choice Center to protest the issue of abortion, or lack of it,” says Pastor Kathy, the group’s leader.

Dressed in the cassock of a Catholic priest, complete with white collar, she’s eager to sum up the church’s views for any potential listeners. “Not having babies is a passive solution. Euthanasia and suicide are for more pro-active go-getters. It’s the ultimate sacrifice, sure, but it’s the best thing you can do to save the planet.”

The demonstrators arrived shortly after 9:00 AM, dressed mostly in black.

Several members of the group rode together in a hearse. They held signs with slogans such as ABORTION FOR A BETTER TOMORROW, SODOMY = WORLD PEACE, and SUICIDE IS THE SOLUTION. One protester, a man wearing an oversized diaper and a skull mask, carried a mobile of dangling baby doll heads.

Reactions from local residents have been mixed.

“I agree with some of it, I guess,” said Rachel, a hairdresser who works down the street. “I’m as pro-choice as they come, but I mean, I’m not sure what they’re going to accomplish. The suicide stuff is just going to make people disagree. It’s weird, but it is more excitement than we usually get around here.”

“It’s all vegan.”

Others had stronger opinions. “It’s disgusting, and they should be ashamed,” said an anonymous resident. “I look down that street and you know what I see? A bunch of sad, angry children marching straight to Hell. Pretty sure a couple are drag queens or whatever they call themselves now. To call themselves a church is a real travesty. And that little dead girl statue is obscene. You put that in the paper.”

Another resident, who goes by Mondo, was more enthusiastic. “Oh, yeah, this is the coolest thing that’s ever happened in this little town,” he said. “There’s this dude over there crawling at the back of the march, oinking like a pig, and all those vampire-looking chicks. It’s metal, bro. That’s all I got to say. That chili they’re giving out is fire, too. I think it’s gotta be like Kobe beef or something.”

Pastor Kathy insists the food is meat-free. “It’s all vegan. Just because we’re anti-human doesn’t mean we’re pro-death.”

By noon, a small crowd of onlookers had gathered across the street from the protest. Very few spoke to demonstrators. Even fewer were brave enough to try the free chili, scooped from a witch’s cauldron by a church member with at least a dozen facial piercings.

“Read their signs,” said Carletta, 14. “They totally poisoned the food. You know they did.”

Visitors to the Women’s Informed Choice Center had no choice but to walk through the protest. Demonstrators were quick to offer them literature. A variety of pamphlets were available, some explaining the church’s views on suicide, others pointing the way to abortion clinics in the area.

“The Women’s Informed Choice Center is a sham,”explained Pastor Kathy. “The name implies it’s a place that supports women, that informs them and gives them choices. They have billboards saying to call them if you’re pregnant and looking for options. Then you get here, they give you an ultrasound and tell you how abortion is definitely the wrong choice. It’s a classic bait and switch operation.”

Afterword

We reached out to the Women’s Informed Choice Center for comment. They emailed us a statement that reads, in part, “We present potentially expectant mothers with a full range of information on all of their choices. Our services are held to high confidentiality standards. We will not discuss the lives or activities of our patients online or in print.”

The protest was broken up by police around 3:00 PM, when the sidewalk outside the Choice Center was sprayed with several gallons of what appeared to be blood. Church members dispersed peacefully.

“It’s corn syrup and food coloring,” said a protester, loading signs into the hearse. “No animals were harmed in the making of this demonstration. Save the animals! Kill yourself!”
